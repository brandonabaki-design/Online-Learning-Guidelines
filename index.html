K-4 Online Learning
Educator Guidelines

A comprehensive framework for delivering engaging, supportive, and structured virtual lessons using Microsoft Teams.

The Golden Rule: Student Wellbeing is Paramount

The emotional and mental wellbeing of our K-4 students comes first. Always maintain a positive, encouraging, and patient tone. Never use phrases that may come off as insensitive, such as "you don't know this," "you are not listening," or "why aren't you paying attention?" Instead, use positive redirection and constructive praise to keep our young learners engaged and feeling safe.

Microsoft Teams Protocol

  • Student Roles (Attendees) Students must be marked as "Attendees" in the meeting options. This prevents them from removing others, muting the teacher, or taking control of the presentation.
  • Microphone Discipline Students should keep their microphones muted by default to minimize background noise. Instruct them clearly on when it is their turn to unmute and speak.
  • Camera Policy (Teachers vs. Students) Teachers: Are required to keep their cameras open to maintain a visual connection (unless a formal request/exemption has been approved).
    Students: Are not required to open their cameras. Respect their privacy and home environments.
  • Managing Persistent Disruptions If a student consistently causes disruptions, log the behavior via an incident report shared with your HOD and Supervisor. Should disruptions continue, you are authorized to temporarily disable the student's microphone and camera. Crucially, you must continue to keep them engaged by enabling their microphone when it is their turn to participate. This measure is not to exclude the student, but to safeguard a positive learning environment for the rest of the class.
  • Safe Session Closure Always use the "End Meeting" feature rather than simply clicking "Leave." You must ensure that all students have exited and the session is completely shut down. This is a critical student safety measure to prevent learners from remaining behind in an unsupervised virtual environment.

Preparation & Delivery

  • Punctuality & Scheduling Be punctual. Scheduled meeting links must be created and shared with students and parents well in advance of the lesson start time.
  • Visual Presentations are Mandatory A presentation (PowerPoint, Sway, interactive whiteboard) is a must at all times. Visual aids are crucial for K-4 learners to comprehend concepts and stay focused.
  • Active Participation & Sharing Ensure students are actively doing work during the lesson. Provide dedicated time for them to share their work, answers, or thoughts in every single lesson.

Administrative Requirements

  • Strict Attendance Tracking Attendance must be accurately recorded for every lesson. Ensure this data is downloaded from Teams and sent to your Head of Department (HOD) promptly after the session concludes.
  • Clear Communication of Links Ensure all meeting links, resources, and assignments are posted in the designated Teams channels clearly so parents and students can easily locate them.

Additional Best Practices

  • Professional Background Ensure your teaching background is neat and distraction-free. Utilize Teams background blur or professional school-approved virtual backgrounds if necessary.
  • Incorporate Brain Breaks K-4 attention spans online are short. Integrate 1-2 minute physical "brain breaks" or stretches into your presentations to keep energy levels up.
  • Tech Patience Technical issues will happen. Remain calm and patient when a student disconnects or struggles with their mic. Your reaction sets the tone for the class.

Pre-Lesson Quick Checklist

Links sent to parents/students?
Visual Presentation ready?
Camera ON, background clean?
Students set to "Attendees"?
Sharing activity planned?
Attendance sheet ready for HOD?
